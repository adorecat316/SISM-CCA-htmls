MUN (Model United Nations) Club

Speak for Nations, Lead with Vision.

Definition, Description & Objectives

Definition

  • Model United Nations (MUN) is an academic simulation of the United Nations where students role-play as delegates representing different countries to discuss global issues.

Description

  • In MUN, students research assigned countries and topics, debate current international problems, propose solutions, negotiate with other delegates, and draft resolutions following formal UN procedures.

Objectives

  • To develop students’ understanding of international relations and global affairs
  • To enhance public speaking, debate, and diplomatic skills
  • To promote critical thinking, research, and problem-solving
  • To build confidence, leadership, and teamwork
  • To encourage global citizenship and awareness of world issues

Suitable Students & Reasons to Join

Suitable for:

  • All Students

Students interested in:

  • Current affairs and world issues
  • Debate, public speaking, and leadership
  • Law, politics, diplomacy, journalism, or international studies

Why Students Should Join:

  • Build confidence in public speaking and expressing ideas
  • Improves English communication and academic writing, research and global issue resolutions
  • Encourages teamwork and respectful disagreement
  • Prepares students for leadership roles and international exposure
  • Strengthens university and scholarship applications

Skills to Be Gained

Students will develop the following key skills:

  • Public Speaking & Debate Skills
  • Research & Analytical Thinking
  • Critical Thinking & Problem-Solving
  • Diplomacy & Negotiation Skills
  • Leadership & Teamwork
  • Time Management & Organization
  • Confidence & Professional Conduct
  • Formal Writing (Position Papers & Resolutions)

Expected Outcomes:

  • Participation in inter-school or international MUN conferences
  • Improved student confidence and academic performance
  • Development of future school leaders and ambassadors,
