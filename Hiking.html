Hiking

One Step at a Time, Reach New Heights.

Hiking club Vision

  • To cultivate resilient, environmentally conscious students who appreciate nature, practice safe outdoor habits, and develop teamwork and self-discipline through regular hiking experiences.
  • The Hiking Club aims to provide meaningful outdoor learning opportunities while prioritising student safety, well-being, and responsible adventure.

Learning Objectives

By participating in the Hiking club, students will:

  • Develop an appreciation for nature and the outdoors
  • Learn basic hiking knowledge, safety awareness, and risk management
  • Build physical endurance and mental resilience
  • Strengthen teamwork, leadership, and communication skills
  • Practice responsibility, self-management, and respect for the environment

3. Objectives

  • To develop students’ basic and advanced netball skills
  • To improve physical fitness, agility, coordination and stamina
  • To build teamwork, communication and cooperation
  • To instill discipline, responsibility and sportsmanship
  • To enhance students’ understanding of netball rules, positions and strategies

Skills to be developed

1. Physical and Practical skills

  • Basic fitness and stamina
  • Proper hiking posture and pacing
  • Use of basic hiking equipment (shoes, backpack, hydration)
  • Navigation awareness (trails, markers, simple maps)

2. Safety and Life skills

  • Risk awareness and hazard identification
  • Weather awareness and decision-making
  • First-aid basics (age-appropriate)
  • Emergency response procedures

3. Personal & Social skills

  • Teamwork & cooperation
  • Communication skills
  • Leadership qualities
  • Discipline & responsibility
  • Confidence & resilience

Membership & Participation Criteria

  • Students with any existing medical conditions (e.g. asthma, heart conditions, recent injuries, or other health concerns) are required to obtain written parental consent before joining the Hiking Club
  • Teachers reserve the right to assess a student’s suitability for each hike based on health, fitness level, and safety considerations

    • Activity Frequency & Scheduling

    • Hiking activities will be conducted once a month, where possible

      • If monthly scheduling is not feasible, activities will be organised once every two months, depending on:

    • School academic calendar
    • Boarding house activities
    • Examination periods

      • All hikes are subject to weather conditions:

    • Hiking activities will be postponed or cancelled in the event of rain or unsafe weather conditions

    Transportation & Capacity

      Initial transport arrangement:

    • 1 school van (maximum 14 students)
    • 1 car (3–4 students)
    • Total estimated capacity per hike: 17–18 students, subject to supervision ratio and safety assessment

      • Club capacity and transport arrangements will be reviewed periodically based on:

    • Student interest
    • Safety considerations
    • Availability of staff and resources

    Sample Activity / Lesson Outline

    1. Pre-Hike Session (School-based)

    • • Introduction to hiking rules and expectations
    • • Safety briefing and basic first-aid awareness
    • • Equipment check and packing essentials
    • • Environmental ethics (Leave No Trace principles)

    2. Hiking Day Activity

    • Warm-up and safety check
    • Guided hike with rest stops
    • Observation tasks (nature awareness, teamwork challenges)
    • Reflection and cool-down session

    3. Post-Hike Reflection

    • Group discussion and sharing
    • Reflection on teamwork, challenges, and learning points
    • Feedback for improvement

    Safety & Supervision

    • Adequate teacher supervision at all times
    • Attendance and headcount conducted before, during, and after the hike
    • Emergency contact details carried by supervising staff
    • Clear stop/cancel criteria for weather, health, or safety concerns

    Future Development

    • Review club size and frequency based on student participation
    • Consider progressive hiking levels (beginner to intermediate)
    • Explore leadership roles for senior students
    • Expand outdoor learning components where appropriate
