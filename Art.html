Slice of Art

Where Imagination Finds Its Canvas.

Slice of Art Vision

  • To cultivate curious, reflective, and resilient students through creative exploration, visual thinking, and collaborative art-making.
  • Slice of Art aims to provide a safe and inclusive space where students can express ideas, develop creative confidence, and engage meaningfully with art beyond the classroom, while fostering discipline, empathy, and respect for diverse perspectives.

Learning Objectives / Goals

By participating in the Slice of Art, students will:

  • Develop confidence in creative expression and visual communication
  • Explore a variety of art media, techniques, and creative processes
  • Build observational skills, imagination, and critical thinking
  • Strengthen collaboration, communication, and peer support
  • Practice reflection, self-discipline, and perseverance through creative challenges
  • Develop appreciation for art, culture, and individual perspectives

Skills to be developed

Creative & Artistic Skills

  • Drawing, painting, mixed media, experimental techniques and sculpture making especially by working on larger scale school or personal projects including mural paintings
  • Visual observation and interpretation
  • Idea generation and concept development
  • Creative problem-solving and risk-taking
  • Understanding of visual elements and composition

Thinking & Learning Skills

  • Reflection and self-assessment
  • Critical and creative thinking
  • Planning and creative decision-making
  • Openness to feedback and revision

Personal & Social Skills

  • Collaboration and teamwork
  • Communication of ideas and intentions
  • Leadership and peer encouragement
  • Confidence, resilience, and patience
  • Respect for materials, shared spaces, and others’ work

Membership & Participation Criteria

  • Slice of Art is open to students with an interest in art and creative exploration, regardless of prior experience
  • Students are expected to participate responsibly, follow instructions, and respect shared materials and spaces
  • For activities such as off-campus sketching, parental consent may be required
  • Teachers reserve the right to assess a student’s suitability for certain activities based on safety, behaviour, and readiness

Activity Frequency & Scheduling

  • Two Slice of Art sessions will be conducted once a week

Session scheduling will take into account:

  • School academic calendar
  • Examination periods

Additional sessions or workshops may be organised for special projects, exhibitions, or collaborations

Venue, Materials & Capacity

• Activities will be conducted primarily in:

  • Art & Design studios
  • Designated creative spaces within the school
  • Selected on-site locations for observational drawing (where appropriate)

• Slice of Art capacity will be determined by:

  • Available space and materials
  • Supervision ratio
  • Nature of the activity

• Materials will be provided where possible; students may be informed in advance if personal materials are required

Sample Activity / Session Outline

Studio-Based Session

  • Guided media exploration and art-making
  • Individual or group work
  • Sharing, discussion, and clean-up

Project-Based Activities (Examples)

  • Collaborative murals or installations
  • Preparation for exhibitions or showcases or other school events

Reflection & Sharing

  • Group discussion or peer feedback
  • Reflection on process, challenges, and learning

Safety & Supervision

  • Adequate teacher supervision at all times
  • Clear instructions given for the safe use of tools and materials
  • Safety guidelines established for materials, equipment, and movement within the studio
  • Attendance taken at the beginning and end of each session
  • Immediate reporting and management of any safety concerns

Future Development

  • • Review club size, structure, and frequency based on student participation and interest
  • • Introduce themed terms (e.g. illustration, sculpture, mixed media, visual storytelling)
  • • Develop leadership roles for senior students (e.g. Art Mentors, Exhibition Assistants)
  • • Organise internal showcases, exhibitions, or community art projects
  • • Explore cross-CCA or interdisciplinary collaborations where appropriate
